subdifferentiable mapping


Let X be a Banach space, and let X* be the dual space of X. For a function f:X→ℝ, and x∈X, let us define

∂⁡f⁢(x)={r*∈X*:f⁢(x)-f⁢(y)≤r∗⁢(x-y)⁢for all⁢y∈X}.

If ∂⁡f⁢(x) is non-empty, then f is subdifferentiable at x∈X, and if ∂⁡f⁢(x) is non-empty for all x, then f is subdifferentiable [1, 2].

  • 2 R.T. Rockafellar, Convex Analysis, Princeton University Press, 1996.
